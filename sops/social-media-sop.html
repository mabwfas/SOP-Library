What is This SOP For?

📱 Your Role as Social Media Manager

As a social media manager, you're responsible for building and engaging the brand's online community. Your job includes:

  • Content creation — Creating posts, reels, stories that attract and engage
  • Scheduling — Planning and posting content consistently
  • Community management — Responding to comments, DMs, and mentions
  • Analytics — Tracking what works and optimizing
  • Growth — Building followers who actually buy
📖

The Instagram Account That Made $0

Why Followers Mean Nothing Without Strategy

A client came to us with an Instagram account. 50,000 followers. Looks impressive, right?

But here's the problem:

  • Posts got 50-100 likes (0.1% engagement rate)
  • Zero DMs asking about products
  • Website traffic from Instagram: almost nothing
  • Sales from Instagram: $0

The previous manager had been buying followers and posting generic content with no strategy.

What we did differently:

  • Stopped chasing followers, started chasing engagement
  • Posted content that answers customer questions
  • Replied to every comment within 1 hour
  • Started Instagram Stories showing behind-the-scenes

Result after 3 months: Followers dropped to 35K (fake ones unfollowed), but engagement hit 5%. Sales from Instagram: $2.4 lakhs.

🎯 The Lesson: 1,000 engaged followers > 100,000 dead followers. Your job isn't to grow vanity metrics — it's to grow SALES.

📱 SOCIAL MEDIA PRINCIPLES

1. ENGAGEMENT OVER FOLLOWERS

A small, engaged audience beats a large, dead audience. Focus on people who actually interact with content.

2. CONSISTENCY IS KING

Posting 3x/week consistently beats posting daily for a month then disappearing. Build a sustainable rhythm.

3. RESPOND WITHIN 1 HOUR

Social media is SOCIAL. If someone comments or DMs, respond fast. Slow responses = lost sales.

4. VALUE FIRST, SELL SECOND

80% of content should provide value (education, entertainment, inspiration). 20% can be promotional.

💡 Platform Rule

Don't try to be everywhere. Master 2-3 platforms where your audience actually is before expanding.

Content Creation

The 80/20 Content Rule

Content Type % of Posts Examples
Value Content 40% Tips, how-tos, education, answers to FAQs
Engagement Content 20% Questions, polls, "this or that", user-generated content
Brand Content 20% Behind-the-scenes, team, story, values
Promotional 20% Product features, sales, launches, offers

Content Pillars (Define 4-5)

📌 What Are Content Pillars?

Content pillars are the main themes you post about. They keep your content focused and on-brand.

Example for a skincare brand:

  • Pillar 1: Skincare education (ingredients, routines)
  • Pillar 2: Product showcases (features, benefits)
  • Pillar 3: Customer results (before/after, testimonials)
  • Pillar 4: Behind-the-scenes (brand story, team)
  • Pillar 5: Lifestyle (self-care, confidence)

Caption Writing Formula

📝 Caption Formula: Hook → Value → CTA

Line 1 (Hook): Stop the scroll. Ask a question or make a bold statement.

"The skincare mistake 90% of people make…"

Lines 2-5 (Value): Deliver the promise. Give them something useful.

"Most people apply products in the wrong order. This is why your $2000 serum isn't working..."

Last Line (CTA): Tell them what to do next.

"Save this post 📌 | Drop a 💧 if you've been doing it wrong!"

Community Engagement

🚨 The 1-Hour Rule

Respond to ALL comments and DMs within 1 hour during business hours. Social media is SOCIAL. Slow responses kill engagement and lose sales.

DM Response Guidelines

Product inquiry <1 hour Detailed, helpful, link to product

Complete LinkedIn Posting Process

📋 Step-by-Step: From Idea to Published Post

  1. Identify the goal — What do we want? (leads, followers, authority)
  2. Choose content type — Portfolio, testimonial, educational, or behind-scenes
  3. Use ChatGPT to brainstorm — (see prompts below)
  4. Write the hook — First line must stop the scroll
  5. Create visuals — Canva carousel or screenshot + metrics
  6. Add hashtags — 3-5 relevant hashtags
  7. Schedule post — Best times: Tue-Thu, 8-10 AM IST
  8. Engage for 30 min — Reply to comments immediately

Handling Difficult Situations

🔴 Negative Comment Goes Viral

🎯 Task Priority Order (What Comes First)

Handle in this order every day:

  1. 🔴 P0 - Crisis/Negative Viral Content → Damage control immediately. Respond within 30 mins.
  2. 🔴 P0 - DMs from Potential Clients → Leads in inbox = money. Respond fast.
  3. 🔴 P0 - Scheduled Post Verification → Make sure today's posts are scheduled correctly.
  4. 🟡 P1 - Comment Engagement → Reply to all comments within 2 hours of posting.
  5. 🟡 P1 - Community Engagement → Like, comment on relevant accounts.
  6. 🟢 P2 - Content Creation → Create posts for upcoming schedule.
  7. 🟢 P2 - Analytics Review → Check what's performing, adjust strategy.
  8. ⚪ P3 - Strategy & Planning → Content calendar, trend research, experiments.

Golden Rule: Crisis > Hot leads > Today's posts > Engagement > Creation > Strategy.

🔴 Negative Comment Goes Viral

Situation: Someone posts a complaint that's getting shared/liked.

Response: Respond quickly and publicly: "We're sorry to hear this. Please DM us your details so we can resolve this directly." Take the detailed conversation private. Never delete unless it's spam/abuse.

Rule: Respond fast. Public acknowledgment + private resolution. Never argue.

🔴 Content Mistake Published

Situation: Typo, wrong image, or incorrect information went live.

Response: If minor: Delete and repost. If already engaged: Edit if possible, or post correction. If major: Acknowledge it with humor if appropriate.

Rule: Fix fast. Don't draw unnecessary attention to small errors.

🔴 Account Engagement is Dropping

Situation: Posts aren't getting likes/comments like they used to.

Response: Analyze: posting times, content types, hashtag performance. Test new formats. Engage more in comments/DMs. Report findings to PM with improvement plan.

Rule: Data first. Don't panic. Test and iterate.

🔴 Client Wants to Post Something Inappropriate

Situation: Client suggests controversial or off-brand content.

Response: "I love the creative thinking! However, this could attract negative attention because [reason]. Can I suggest an alternative that achieves the same goal?"

Rule: Advise against. Document if they insist. Never post anything unethical.

🔴 Competitor Copying Our Content

Situation: Competitor is using similar content/ideas.

Response: Document with screenshots. Report to PM. Stay focused on our content being better. If it's clear plagiarism, consider platform's copyright reporting.

Rule: Don't engage publicly. Focus on staying ahead, not fighting behind.

🔴 No Content Ideas Left

Situation: Creative block — you're out of ideas.

Response: Review what worked before. Check competitor accounts. Ask ChatGPT for 20 content ideas in your niche. Repurpose old content in new formats. Take a short break.

Rule: Everyone gets stuck. Use tools and templates. Batch planning prevents this.

🔴 Account Was Hacked/Compromised

Situation: Unauthorized access or strange activity on account.

Response: Immediately: Change password, enable 2FA, check connected apps, remove unauthorized access. Alert management. Review what was posted/messaged. Report to platform if needed.

Rule: Security first. Then damage control. Document everything.

🔴 Client Expects Viral Results Immediately

Situation: Client wants "viral" posts after one week.

Response: "Going viral is unpredictable. What IS predictable is consistent growth through strategic content. Let's look at our week-over-week metrics instead."

Rule: Set realistic expectations. Consistency beats virality long-term.
